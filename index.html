BS Thesis Research

Evaluation of Microbial and Heavy Metal Contaminants in Local and Imported Packaged Biscuits Available in Islamabad, Pakistan

Bahria University Islamabad | Apr 2019 ‚Äì Sep 2019

Research Methodology:
  • Collected and analyzed 30 biscuit samples (15 local, 15 imported) to evaluate microbial contamination and heavy metal concentrations (nickel, cadmium, chromium, lead)
  • Conducted microbial assays using Nutrient Agar, EMB Agar, and S.S Agar
  • Applied Atomic Absorption Spectrophotometry (AAS) for heavy metal detection
  • Performed statistical analysis using SPSS to assess contamination trends and compliance with WHO and Pakistan food safety standards
Key Findings & Public Health Impact:

Identified excessive nickel and chromium contamination in several samples, highlighting significant food safety risks and public health implications. Research contributed to understanding of food contamination patterns in Pakistan's packaged food industry.
